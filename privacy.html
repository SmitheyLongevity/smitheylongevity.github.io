Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 2026  ·  Last updated: June 2026

This Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy explains how Smithey Longevity Group LLC ("SLG," "we," "us") collects, uses, shares, and protects consumer health data. It is provided to comply with Washington State's My Health My Data Act (MHMDA) and applies to the consumer health data we process about the people we work with. It supplements any other terms or privacy notices we provide.

SLG provides educational and analytical wellness services. We are not a healthcare provider, and nothing we do is medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Please consult a licensed clinician for medical concerns.

1. What this policy covers

"Consumer health data" means personal information that is linked or reasonably linkable to you and that identifies your past, present, or future physical or mental health status. This policy covers that data. We collect it only with your consent and use it to provide you with personalized, educational wellness analysis and recommendations.

2. Categories of consumer health data we collect

  • Information you give us in our intake questionnaire: health history, conditions, medications and supplements, injuries, allergies, sleep, nutrition, movement and exercise, stress and wellbeing, social connection, goals, and related lifestyle information.
  • Wearable and device data from services you connect, such as Oura and Apple Health: sleep, heart rate, heart-rate variability, activity, and related metrics.
  • Lab or biomarker results that you choose to share with us.
  • Basic identifiers needed to deliver the service and respond to you, such as your name and email address.

3. Where we collect it from

  • Directly from you (forms, messages, documents you provide).
  • From wearable and health platforms you authorize us to connect to (for example, Oura and Apple Health).
  • From laboratory or diagnostic results you choose to share.

4. How and why we use your data

We use your consumer health data to:

  • Analyze your baseline, identify the key drivers of your goals, and build personalized, educational recommendations and protocols for you.
  • Measure your progress over time and adjust your recommendations.
  • Communicate with you about your engagement.

Identified vs. de-identified use

To provide your personalized service, we necessarily use data that identifies you. Separately, and only with your optional consent, we may use de-identified data (with your name and identifying details removed) to improve our methods and for aggregate analysis. We do not attempt to re-identify de-identified data, and we require anyone who receives it to do the same.

5. How we share your data

We share consumer health data only as needed to provide the service, and only with your consent. We share with the following categories of third parties and service providers:

  • Wearable and health platforms you connect (for example, Oura Health and Apple), to retrieve your data.
  • Technology and infrastructure providers that host our forms and store data securely (for example, Google Workspace / Google Forms).
  • Secure data-analysis and AI tools used to analyze data; where these tools are used to improve our methods or for aggregate work, the data provided to them is de-identified.
  • Laboratory or diagnostic providers, if you engage them through us.
  • Professional advisors (such as legal counsel) and authorities where required by law.

We do not sell your consumer health data, and we do not share it for advertising or for any purpose you have not consented to.

6. Consent

We collect your consumer health data only after you give clear, opt-in consent. We ask for separate consent to (a) collect and use your data to provide your service, (b) share it with the service providers needed to deliver it, (c) use de-identified data to improve our methods (optional), and (d) use anonymized results as an example (optional). You may withdraw any consent at any time, as described below.

7. How long we keep your data

We keep your consumer health data only as long as needed to provide your service and for legitimate, disclosed purposes, and then we delete or de-identify it. You can request deletion at any time (see your rights below).

8. How we protect your data

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards, including encryption in transit and at rest where available, access controls, and limiting access to those who need it. No system is perfectly secure, but we work to protect your data and to limit what we collect to what we need.

9. De-identification

Where we de-identify data, we take reasonable measures to ensure it cannot be linked back to you, we publicly commit not to re-identify it, and we contractually require recipients to honor the same commitment.

10. Your rights

Under MHMDA, you have the right to:

  • Access the consumer health data we have about you, and confirm whether we are processing it.
  • Obtain a list of the third parties and affiliates with whom we have shared your data.
  • Withdraw consent to our collection and sharing of your data.
  • Delete your consumer health data.

To exercise any of these rights, email andrew@smitheylongevity.com. We will verify your request and respond within 45 days as required by law (we aim to act sooner), and we will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights. If we deny a request, you may appeal by replying to our response.

11. No sale of consumer health data

We do not sell consumer health data, and we will not do so without the separate, valid authorization that the law requires.

12. Children

Our services are for adults. We do not knowingly collect consumer health data from anyone under 18.

13. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy. We will post the updated version here with a new effective date, and where required we will obtain your consent before using previously collected data for materially new purposes.

14. Contact us

Questions or requests: Smithey Longevity Group LLC, andrew@smitheylongevity.com.
